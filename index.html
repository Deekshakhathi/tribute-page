Shakuntala Devi

(4 November 1929 – 21 April 2013) was an Indian mental calculator and writer, popularly known as the "Human Computer". Her talent earned her a place in the 1982 edition of The Guinness Book of World Records. However, the certificate for the record was given posthumously on 30 July 2020, despite Devi achieving her world record on 18 June 1980 at Imperial College, London. Devi was a precocious child and she demonstrated her arithmetic abilities at the University of Mysore without any formal education.

Guinness World Records: Shakuntala Devi's remarkable mental calculation skills led her to enter the Guinness Book of World Records. In 1980, she successfully multiplied two 13-digit numbers within seconds, beating the computer's time of 62 seconds. Her achievement remains unparalleled to this day.


Mathematical Genius: Devi's talent extended beyond mental calculations. She possessed a profound understanding of mathematical concepts and principles. Despite receiving no formal education, she became an authority in the field of mathematics and its applications.


Writing Career: Devi authored several books on mathematics, puzzles, and astrology. Her notable works include "Fun with Numbers," "Astrology for You," and "Puzzles to Puzzle You." Her books aimed to make mathematics more accessible and enjoyable for a wider audience.
